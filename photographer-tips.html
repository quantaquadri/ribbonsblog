4 Tips For Finding Awesome Reliable Photographers

Wedding Planning 19 Jan, 2018

Image Source: Google Photos

Having conducted interviews with a couple of people who have had their fair share of photographer dissapointments, here are the key lessons they wished they had done before hiring photographers for their events.

1. Photography Style

Everyone has that thing that tickles their fancy, how you would want to appear in a fantasy world, how you would want the background or senere of your photos to look of the angle you would want to be captured. Trust me it’s not a great idea for you to micromanage a photographer to that for you on your wedding day, you would be too distracted with so many things that you definitely won't be able to orchestrate the shots. So to get it right from the get go, find that photographer that just dance to your rhythm, that one that sort of gets your style. Look for his works and see if he has it.

2. Review and Recommendations

You see this one I can’t overemphasize, I swear, this is probably the most important of all you need to get an awesome photographer. This is because some of the works photographers put on social media are their best works. I have had brides tell me that they pay top cash for a big brand photographers(“celebrity photographer”) only to get really crappy work in the end. It turns out the carefully select pictures they put online and they go on ruining the best moments in the life of a couple. We are working on something to resolve this, you want to get updates? Don’t just use the instagram photos to judge the photographer. Rather use instagram to find the couples they have worked with before and get feedback on how they performed and the quality of work delivered

Image Source: Google Photos

3. Don’t just use instagram as reference

I must have stated it subtly above, but I feel it deserves a heading of it’s own. Don’t be swayed by the glamour of instagram, use that as a platform to select possible candidate. Ribbons is working something that can help you compare, see real-time vetted recommendations and review and also see pricing. But yes please compare their works, find your style and start conversations with at least 3 at a time.

See this story about how a photographer someone found via Instagram who ended up runing her wedding day.

4. Ask a lot of questions

Ask a whole lot of questions; About the rights to the photos?, How much for every detail, soft copy, hard copies, framed copies? How many images should I expect? Will they be high resolution or low resolution? How long it will take to have them all delivered to you? Just askas many questions as possible and get al the clarity you need.
